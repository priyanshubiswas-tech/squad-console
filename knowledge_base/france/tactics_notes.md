# France Internal Tactics Dossier — Staff Only

**Classification:** Internal squad/staff use only. Not for public release.
**Manager:** Didier Deschamps
**Last updated context:** Post 3-0 win vs. Sweden (2026-06-30, World Cup)

## Deschamps' Managerial Philosophy

Deschamps has managed France since 2012 and built his entire body of work — Euro 2000 as a player, 1998 and 2018 World Cups as player/manager, plus the 2016 and 2022 finals — on pragmatism over aesthetics. He is a results-first coach: he will happily cede possession and territory if it means denying the opponent central passing lanes, then strike through a smaller number of high-quality transition moments. Internally we describe his approach as "controlled reaction" — a mid-to-low block, compact banks of four and five, and quick vertical combinations the moment the ball is won back. He is not dogmatic about a single system; he has flexed between a back four and a back three/five throughout his tenure depending on personnel fitness and the phase of a tournament, and he privileges tournament-hardened, mentally reliable players over in-form domestic-league form. Squad harmony and discipline are non-negotiable for him — he has dropped in-form players before for disciplinary or fit reasons, and staff should expect that to continue this cycle.

## Formation Options

**Option A — 4-2-3-1 (base/default):** Maignan or Samba in goal; back four of Gusto/Koundé (RB), Konaté, Upamecano or Saliba (CB pairing), Théo Hernandez (LB); double pivot of Tchouaméni and Koné (or Zaïre-Emery); Dembélé and Olise as inverted wide forwards either side of an attacking midfielder (Zaïre-Emery or Doué); Mbappé as the central striker/false 9. This is our most-used shape and matches Deschamps' preference for defensive solidity through the middle with Tchouaméni screening the back line.

**Option B — 3-4-1-2 / back-five variant:** Used against low blocks or when we want both full-backs high. Konaté, Upamecano, Saliba across the back three; Théo Hernandez and Gusto (or Koundé) as wing-backs; Tchouaméni and Koné central; Zaïre-Emery in the 10 slot; Mbappé and Thuram or Olise as a front two. Gives Théo license to bomb forward knowing Saliba covers the left channel behind him.

**Option C — 4-3-3 with Dembélé/Mbappé/Olise front line:** A more expansive option for must-win games against mid-tier opposition, with Rabiot or Kanté-style legs (in practice now Koné) providing the extra central body and Doué rotating in off the bench to change tempo.

## Key-Player Dependencies

- **Mbappé absent:** We lose our only reliable individual chance-creator in isolation (1v1 in behind) and our captaincy/leadership focal point. Olise and Dembélé must combine more centrally; expect a drop in directness and in penalty-box occupation. Thuram or Barcola slot in as the central point but neither replicates Mbappé's acceleration over 30+ yards.
- **Tchouaméni absent:** The double pivot loses its main shield in front of the back four; Koné or Zaïre-Emery has to sit deeper than natural, exposing transition defense against fast counters.
- **Théo Hernandez absent:** Our primary source of left-sided width and crossing volume disappears; Digne is a like-for-like positional deputy but offers less explosive overlap pace, so wide combinations with Dembélé/Olise slow down.
- **Koundé absent:** Loses ball-progression from right-back into midfield; Gusto is athletically comparable but less experienced reading when to invert into midfield.
- **Konaté absent:** Back line loses its primary pace-and-power central defender against direct opposition strikers; Upamecano/Saliba pairing is more comfortable on the ball but more exploitable in behind.

## Pressing & Defensive Shape

Mid-block as default, engaging higher only after a clear trigger (poor first touch, backwards pass, wide areas near the touchline). Front three/one press in a curved shape to force play down the channels rather than through the center. Tchouaméni and the second pivot stay compact and refuse to be split — this is the single most important defensive instruction of the setup. Wide forwards (Dembélé, Olise) are expected to track full-backs on the recovery run, which occasionally leaves them fatigued in the final 20 minutes of matches, particularly Dembélé given his injury history.

## Set-Piece Notes (Internal)

- **Attacking corners:** Konaté and Upamecano (or Saliba) as principal near/far post targets; Olise's delivery quality (both feet, genuine dead-ball threat) makes him first-choice corner and free-kick taker over Mbappé in wide areas.
- **Defensive corners:** Zonal-man hybrid; Maignan commands the six-yard box, Tchouaméni marks the opponent's most dangerous aerial threat man-to-man.
- **Short free-kick routines:** Reserved for Mbappé/Olise combination just outside the box, using Doué as a decoy runner when he's on the pitch.

## Squad Depth & Rotation by Position

- **GK:** Maignan (1), Samba (2, experienced Ligue 1 shot-stopper), Risser (3, young developing talent, Lens).
- **RB:** Gusto and Koundé (Koundé can also play CB), giving genuine rotation without drop-off.
- **CB:** Konaté, Upamecano, Saliba, with Lacroix as capable depth (Crystal Palace, solid positionally) and Digne able to cover emergency LB/LCB in a back three.
- **LB:** Théo Hernandez first choice, Digne experienced deputy, Lucas Hernandez (PSG, returning from injuries) as a further option who can also play CB.
- **DM/CM:** Tchouaméni and Koné as the first-choice pivot; Zaïre-Emery for tempo/energy; Rabiot for aerial presence and legs in big games; Kanté as a late-game or must-win tactical option — his engine is undiminished but starts have been managed given his age and the Fenerbahçe schedule.
- **Wide/attacking forward:** Olise, Dembélé, Doué, Barcola, Akliouche and Cherki give real rotation depth and different profiles (Olise for end product, Doué/Cherki for dribbling and unpredictability off the bench).
- **CF:** Mbappé undisputed first choice; Thuram as a genuine alternative/partner (more of a target/pressing forward); Mateta as an aerial/physical plan-B option from the bench.

## Honest Tactical Weakness

The double pivot's defensive solidity depends heavily on Tchouaméni's positioning discipline; when he is pulled out of position or subbed off, France have historically been vulnerable to quick central combinations and runners from deep — a fast, technical midfield three (the kind Spain or an in-form Belgium/Portugal midfield can field) can overload that zone before the back four resets. Additionally, our first-choice center-back pairing skews toward power and pace over composed possession, meaning sustained high pressing from an opponent can occasionally force Upamecano into rushed or rash decisions in possession.
