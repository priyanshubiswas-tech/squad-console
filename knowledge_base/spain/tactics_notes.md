# Spain Tactical Dossier — Internal Staff Use Only

*Confidential. Prepared for coaching staff and squad management. Not for public or media distribution.*

## De la Fuente's Philosophy & Managerial History

Luis de la Fuente built his Euro 2024-winning side on the possession-based, positionally disciplined identity Spain has carried since the tiki-taka era, but with two modern additions: a genuine high press and a willingness to be pragmatic. Spain circulate the ball patiently through midfield, prioritize third-man combinations and quick one-touch exchanges in tight areas, and defend from the front with a coordinated, largely man-oriented press. Unlike some purist predecessors, de la Fuente has shown he will cede possession when it suits the scoreline or opponent, and he is comfortable making in-game structural changes rather than trusting the model blindly. Spain went unbeaten through Euro 2024, becoming the first side to win seven games at a European Championship without needing a penalty shootout, and did so with a settled spine (Rodri as the single pivot, Fabián Ruiz and a rotating second eight, Yamal and Nico Williams inverting off the flanks). That template — 4-3-3 in possession, compact mid-block out of it — remains our baseline heading into the World Cup.

## Formation Options

**Option A — Established 4-3-3 (Euro 2024 template).** GK: Raya. Back four: Pubill (RB), Eric García (RCB), Laporte (LCB), Grimaldo (LB). Pivot: Rodri. Eights: Pedri and Fabián Ruiz. Front three: Lamine Yamal (RW, inverting), Nico Williams (LW, inverting), with Oyarzabal or Ferran Torres as the central point.

**Option B — Youth-forward variant.** GK: Raya. Back four: Pubill, Cubarsí, Eric García, Cucurella. Pivot: Zubimendi (in for Rodri to rest legs against a lower block). Eights: Pedri, Gavi. Front three: Yamal, Nico Williams, Torres. Higher energy, higher press intensity, less experience in the spine.

**Option C — Control-and-chaos hybrid vs. physical opponents.** GK: Raya. Back four: Porro, Laporte, Eric García, Grimaldo. Double pivot: Rodri + Fabián Ruiz sitting deeper than usual (effectively a 4-2-3-1). Álex Baena as the free number 10 behind Oyarzabal, with Yamal and Dani Olmo/Nico Williams as the wide outlets. Gives more defensive cover against transition-heavy sides while retaining Baena's set-piece and half-space creativity.

## Key-Player Dependencies

- **Rodri absent:** this is our single biggest structural risk. No other player in the pool combines his tempo-control, press-resistance under pressure, and defensive positioning. Zubimendi is the closest like-for-like (more disciplined, less range of passing); Merino can deputize but is a converted 8, not a true single pivot, and leaves the back four more exposed on turnovers.
- **Pedri absent:** we lose our primary progressor between the lines. Fabián Ruiz can carry more of the creative load but is more effective arriving late into the box than dictating tempo from deep. Gavi's game is more about energy and duels than Pedri's press-resistant control, so overall tempo drops noticeably.
- **Lamine Yamal absent:** removes our highest end-product 1v1 threat and our main way of manufacturing shots from nothing in low-block games. Yéremy Pino or Ferran Torres can rotate into the right-wing role but neither offers Yamal's combination of dribbling under pressure and end product at his age.
- **Grimaldo absent:** we lose our best dead-ball delivery from the left and a genuine attacking outlet at left-back; Cucurella is more defensively combative but considerably less creative going forward.

## Pressing & Defensive Shape

High defensive line, coordinated man-oriented press triggered from the front three — wingers show the opposition full-backs inside or outside depending on the plan, forwards curve their runs to block the pass back to the center-backs. Midfield presses man-to-man onto opposition pivots once the trigger is on, which is high-risk/high-reward: it wins the ball high but leaves space in behind the back four if the initial press is beaten. Out of possession without the ball won back quickly, Spain drop into a compact mid-block, staying narrow through the center and inviting play wide before pressing the ball on the touchline.

## Set-Piece Notes (Internal Analysis)

Corners from the left are usually whipped in by Grimaldo (or Baena when on the pitch) targeting the near-post run, with Laporte and Pubill the primary aerial targets given their height and jumping ability. From the right, Porro or Oyarzabal's deliveries tend to look for a cutback zone at the penalty spot rather than a direct near-post ball. On defensive set pieces, zonal marking at the six-yard box supplemented by man-marking on the opposition's most dangerous aerial threat is the preferred approach — this is an area to keep drilling given the squad's average height at center-back is not a standout strength.

## Squad Depth & Rotation

- **GK:** Raya (first choice on current form), Unai Simón (long-time incumbent, still a viable starter and squad leader), Joan Garcia (developing third option).
- **Center-back:** Eric García, Laporte, Cubarsí — good balance of experience (Laporte) and ball-playing composure (Cubarsí, Eric García), though this remains a thinner department than we'd like.
- **Full-back:** Pubill and Porro on the right (Marcos Llorente as a versatile alternate who can also play centrally); Grimaldo and Cucurella on the left.
- **Pivot/six:** Rodri, Zubimendi, with Merino as an emergency option.
- **Attacking midfield:** Pedri, Gavi, Fabián Ruiz, Álex Baena, Dani Olmo — excellent depth and interchangeable profiles.
- **Wide forwards:** Lamine Yamal, Nico Williams, Yéremy Pino, Ferran Torres.
- **Central striker:** Oyarzabal (also plays left-sided), Borja Iglesias (target-man alternative), Víctor Muñoz (emerging option).

## Individual Player Traits

- **David Raya** — comfortable playing out from the back under pressure, good distribution range, calm decision-making in build-up.
- **Marc Pubill** — very athletic, strong in the air for a full-back, good recovery pace, effective going forward on the overlap.
- **Pau Cubarsí** — composed on the ball for his age, reads passing lanes well, comfortable stepping into midfield with the ball.
- **Aymeric Laporte** — experienced, strong aerially, good long-range passing to switch play; has lost half a yard of pace with age.
- **Álex Grimaldo** — left-footed, excellent dead-ball delivery, attacking instincts from full-back, less combative in individual defensive duels.
- **Marc Cucurella** — high work-rate, tenacious in tackles, more defensively reliable than creative.
- **Rodri** — exceptional passing range and tempo control, positionally intelligent, not a burst-pace player so relies on anticipation to cover ground.
- **Pedri** — press-resistant in tight spaces, excellent one- and two-touch combination play, sets the team's tempo.
- **Lamine Yamal** — left-footed, plays off the right to cut inside, elite dribbling and end product for his age, still developing defensive work rate.
- **Nico Williams** — right-footed, plays off the left to cut inside, explosive acceleration and direct 1v1 dribbling.
- **Mikel Oyarzabal** — left-footed, intelligent movement in the box, reliable penalty and dead-ball taker, scored the Euro 2024 final winner.
- **Álex Baena** — creative left-footed set-piece specialist, comfortable operating as a free 10 in half-spaces.

## Honest Tactical Weakness

The high defensive line, combined with a center-back pool that is either young and inexperienced at this level (Cubarsí) or has lost a step of recovery pace (Laporte), is the clearest exploitable weakness. A well-organized opponent with a fast, direct front line that plays in behind rather than trying to build through our press could expose the space in behind the back four before our pivot and full-backs can recover — this is the scenario we most need to game-plan against.
